# Blueprint 1: The Home Wall

## Purpose

The Home Wall is the visual representation of a human life. It is not a dashboard, menu, or control panel.

## Emotional Objective

The user should feel calm, curious, and connected.

The Home Wall should feel less like opening software and more like standing quietly in front of your own life.

## Behaviour

- Display one brick per year.
- Past years are warm and present.
- The current year is subtly illuminated.
- Future years are softly visible and represent possibility.
- The last visible years fade into possibility rather than ending abruptly.
- No statistics appear.
- No badges, notifications, gamification, advertisements, or progress bars appear.
- Search is not part of the Home Wall.
- AI remains invisible.

## Interaction

- Selecting a brick opens the corresponding year.
- The transition is calm and brief.
- The wall does not bounce, pulse, or pretend to breathe.
- Silence is part of the experience.

## Design Principle

Negative space is deliberate. The absence of unnecessary controls is a feature.
