# BP001 Illustrated Blueprint: The Home Wall

## Status

Approved architectural direction. Illustrated version 1.

## Prime Sentence

> The Home Wall should feel less like opening software and more like standing quietly in front of your own life.

## Illustration

![BP001 Home Wall Illustrated Blueprint](BP001_Home_Wall_Illustrated_Blueprint.png)

## Purpose

The Home Wall is the visual representation of a human life.

It is not a dashboard, a menu, a control panel, a productivity screen, or an analytics page.

It is the calm front door to Me-Wall.

## Emotional Objective

The user should feel, in order:

1. Calm
2. Curious
3. Connected

The Home Wall must never feel busy, childish, gamified, clinical, or technical.

## Layout

The wall occupies almost the entire screen.

The interface contains only what is necessary:

- the wall of years
- a discreet title or identity marker
- minimal navigation
- no visible AI controls
- no dashboard panels
- no statistics

## Bricks

Each brick represents one year of the person's life.

Every brick has identical dimensions.

No year is visually more important than another.

## Past, Present, and Future

### Past Years

Past years are warm and present. They should feel lived-in, not archived.

### Current Year

The current year is subtly illuminated. It should feel alive and still unfolding.

### Future Years

Future years remain softly visible. They represent possibility, not absence.

The final visible years fade gently into possibility rather than ending abruptly.

## Interaction

### Hover or Focus

When the user pauses over a brick, Me-Wall may show a tiny contextual hint:

- year
- age
- short memory invitation

It must not show statistics.

### Selection

Selecting a brick opens the corresponding year view.

The transition should be calm and brief.

Maximum transition duration: 500 milliseconds.

## Prohibited Elements

The Home Wall must never display:

- advertising
- badges
- notifications
- red warning circles
- achievement trophies
- streaks
- progress bars
- leaderboards
- productivity metrics
- total memory counts
- storage statistics

## Animation

Animation is allowed only when it supports understanding.

The wall does not bounce, pulse, breathe, flash, or perform decorative motion.

## Sound

The Home Wall is silent.

The only permitted sound in Me-Wall is user-controlled memory playback, such as voice recordings or video.

## Accessibility

The Home Wall must support:

- mouse
- touch
- keyboard
- screen readers
- high zoom
- older users
- users with low vision
- users with motor impairment

Every brick must be reachable without a mouse.

## Acceptance Criteria

The Home Wall is acceptable when:

1. A user immediately understands that each brick represents a year.
2. The screen feels calm before it feels functional.
3. There are no statistics, badges, notifications, or gamification.
4. Selecting a brick feels like opening a room in one’s own life.
5. Future years feel hopeful rather than empty.
6. The interface is usable without instructions.
7. The screen remains dignified for a user aged 18, 68, or 91.

## Architect's Note

Negative space is not missing functionality.

It is restraint.

The Home Wall must create a place where attention settles.
