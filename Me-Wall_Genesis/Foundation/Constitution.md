# The Me-Wall Constitution

## Prime Directive

Every decision must preserve, deepen, or honour a person's relationship with their own life. If it does none of these, it does not belong in Me-Wall.

## Foundational Principles

1. The individual is always at the centre.
2. Memories are sacred.
3. Nothing interrupts a memory being told.
4. The software adapts to people, never the other way around.
5. AI serves the storyteller. It never replaces them.
6. The user remembers. Me-Wall remembers the connections.
7. Every feature must help someone feel closer to their own life.
8. Memories are living things. They are never completed, only continued.
9. The user owns their life story and must always be able to export it.
10. Silence, patience, dignity, and restraint are part of the interface.

## Founder’s Promise

We will never ask people to adapt their lives to our software. We will adapt our software to the way people naturally remember, reflect, and tell their stories.

Every feature, every screen, and every interaction must honour the individual, preserve their dignity, and help them feel closer to their own life.

If ever we must choose between technical elegance and human understanding, we will choose the human being.
